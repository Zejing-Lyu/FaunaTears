About Us

OUR MISSION

is to ensure the endemic species in Australia, are secured and recovering in the wild.

Why it matters

According to the report presented by the Australian Conservation Foundation, Australia has the most extinct species in the world. Based on research, it has been proved that colonisation has caused 29 species to be extinct. Moreover, Australia has more than 1500 endangered species and ecological communities that are threatened. Unfortunately, the government has spotted and taken steps to support just five habitats so far. However, it is critical to protect the endangered species by taking up actions that prevent or stops the causes that lead to species extinction.

Our Goal

Our goal is to develop a website that predicts the species that are close to extinction, compare their geospatial data with the environmental destruction data and educate the user by providing prominent insights about what is leading them to the path of extinction, and also directs them to perform activities/services to conserve them which are organised by other organisations in Australia.

We use population data to predict the species that are close to extinction in Australia. Providing information about those endangered species and the visualization of the population trend over the years. Help you learn more about the rare, endemic species in Australia.
