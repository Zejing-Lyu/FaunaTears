About Us

Our Mission

To provide inghts to volunteers on the various endangered species in Australia and help them to prevent the species from being extinct.

Why it matters

According to the report presented by the Australian Conservation Foundation, Australia has the most extinct species in the world. Based on research, it has been proved that colonisation has caused 29 species to be extinct. Moreover, Australia has more than 1500 endangered species and ecological communities that are threatened. Unfortunately, the government has spotted and taken steps to support just five habitats so far. However, it is critical to protect the endangered species by taking up actions that prevent or stops the causes that lead to species extinction.

Our Goal

Fauna Tears predicts the species that are close to extinction, compare their geospatial data with the environmental destruction data and educate the user by providing prominent insights about what is leading them to the path of extinction, and also directs them to perform activities/services to conserve them which are organised by other organisations in Australia. Fauna Tears aims to target the people who have intentions to conserve wildlife by learning about conservation practices and be a volunteer to save the endangered species from extinction.
